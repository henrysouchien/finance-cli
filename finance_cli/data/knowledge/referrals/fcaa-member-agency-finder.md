# FCAA Member Agency Finder

The Financial Counseling Association of America (FCAA) is the second major nonprofit credit-counseling certification body in the US, alongside NFCC. FCAA was founded in 1993 (originally as AICCCA — Association of Independent Consumer Credit Counseling Agencies — rebranded to FCAA in 2014). Like NFCC, FCAA does not provide counseling directly; it certifies and accredits a network of member agencies that deliver counseling services across the country.

FCAA member agencies are 501(c)(3) nonprofits offering **debt management plans (DMPs)**, **budget and money management counseling**, **housing counseling** (foreclosure prevention, pre-purchase, reverse-mortgage), **student loan counseling**, and **bankruptcy pre-filing and post-filing counseling** required under BAPCPA. Fees are typically nominal or sliding-scale; some services are free. The member-agency finder at fcaa.org connects users to their nearest accredited agency.

## Use When

Surface FCAA as a referral when:

- The client needs a **debt management plan** but doesn't qualify for a consolidation loan on reasonable terms — FCAA member agencies negotiate similar rate reductions to NFCC member agencies.
- The client is considering **debt settlement** but is uneasy about for-profit settlement companies — FCAA accreditation + nonprofit status is a legitimacy marker on par with NFCC.
- The client is **pre-bankruptcy** and needs the BAPCPA-required pre-filing credit counseling course or post-filing financial management course from an approved nonprofit (FCAA member agencies are commonly approved by the U.S. Trustee).
- The client is **facing foreclosure** and needs HUD-certified housing counseling — many FCAA member agencies are also HUD-approved.
- The client has tried NFCC referrals and the geographic match wasn't a fit (FCAA's member-agency footprint is independent of NFCC's; offering both gives the client more options).
- The client suspects they're being targeted by a **debt-management or credit-repair scam** — FCAA's member-agency directory is one of the easier ways to verify a legitimate alternative.

Distinguish FCAA member agencies from for-profit "credit repair" or "debt settlement" companies: FCAA accreditation + nonprofit status + nominal fees are the legitimacy markers. NFCC and FCAA are typically named together when surfacing nonprofit-counseling alternatives.
