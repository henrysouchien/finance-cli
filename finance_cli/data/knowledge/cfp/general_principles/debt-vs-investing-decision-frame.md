# Debt vs. Investing Decision Frame

Debt payoff and investing compete for the same marginal dollar. The useful question is not "is investing good?" It is "given this user's debt rates, reserve, surplus, time horizon, and account options, which use of the next dollar is most defensible?"

This topic is a sequencing frame. It does not recommend securities, target returns, asset allocations, or trades.

## The Sequencing Ladder

Use the ladder as a diagnostic, not a rigid universal rule.

1. **Keep required minimums current.** Missing required debt payments can create fees, credit damage, default risk, or legal escalation.
2. **Build a starter reserve when cash is thin.** A small shock can become new debt if there is no cash buffer.
3. **Pay down high-interest debt.** Investor.gov describes eliminating high-interest debt as a strong pre-investing priority. Use the current APR, balance, and payment facts rather than guessing.
4. **Capture clearly available employer match if affordable.** Match can be valuable, but only if plan eligibility, vesting, payroll timing, and cash-flow impact are known.
5. **Use tax-advantaged contribution room when facts are known.** IRA, workplace-plan, and HSA decisions depend on tax year, compensation, filing status, eligibility, phaseouts, and plan rules.
6. **Fund taxable investing only after near-term risks are handled.** Taxable brokerage funding is most defensible when the cash is long-horizon, reserves are adequate, debt is controlled, and the user understands the investment-selection boundary.

## Comparing Returns

Debt payoff produces a known avoided-interest result. Investing produces uncertain future returns and possible losses.

The coach should compare:

- debt APR and whether interest is tax-advantaged;
- minimum payment, accelerated payment, and payoff date;
- emergency reserve gap;
- surplus available after required bills and minimums;
- employer match availability;
- tax-advantaged room and user tax facts;
- time horizon and liquidity need.

Use `advisory_debt_vs_invest` and `advisory_contribution_priority` when the relevant inputs are available. Show assumptions. Do not present a market-return assumption as guaranteed.

## Readiness Outcomes

**Fix-first debt.** High-interest debt dominates the next dollar. Route to `general_principles.debt-reduction-strategies`.

**Fix-first reserve.** Emergency cash is too thin. Route to emergency-fund planning before account funding.

**Match/data-needed.** Workplace match or contribution room may be relevant, but plan or tax facts are missing.

**Long-horizon funding-ready.** Debt, reserve, and cash-flow checks are acceptable, and the user has a long-horizon destination account. Funding can be framed as cash movement only.

**Refer.** The user asks for security selection, allocation, rollover, tax execution, or regulated investment advice.

## Common Pitfalls

- Comparing a certain debt APR to an optimistic market return as if both were certain.
- Ignoring minimum payments while optimizing the next dollar.
- Treating all debt the same regardless of APR, collateral, tax treatment, or default risk.
- Skipping the emergency-reserve check.
- Funding taxable investing while employer match or IRA facts are unknown.
- Letting a debt-vs-investing answer become a portfolio recommendation.
