# Retirement Income Sources

Retirement income readiness starts by listing possible income sources and the
facts still missing from each source. The coach can help the user inventory
sources, identify data gaps, and prepare questions for professionals. It does
not decide claiming dates, pension elections, annuity purchases, withdrawal
orders, investment allocations, or tax filing positions.

Use this topic when a user asks whether they can retire, how their paycheck
might be replaced, or which documents they should gather before meeting a
fiduciary planner or CPA.

## Core Sources

**Social Security is a lifetime benefit source.** The user needs their own SSA
estimate, work history, claimed/unclaimed status, and household context before
any planning conversation is meaningful. The coach may explain claiming
vocabulary, but not choose a claiming age.

**Defined-benefit pensions need plan facts.** Pension estimates, survivor
options, lump-sum offers, early-retirement reductions, and COLA terms come from
plan documents or the plan administrator. The coach can list questions; the plan
administrator, fiduciary, CPA, or attorney handles interpretation and election
decisions.

**Defined-contribution plans create account balances, not guaranteed income.**
401(k), 403(b), 457(b), TSP, SIMPLE, SEP, solo 401(k), IRA, Roth IRA, and
taxable investment balances can support retirement spending, but market risk,
fees, taxation, withdrawal timing, and RMD rules change the result.

**Annuities may convert assets into contract income.** Existing annuity
contracts and proposed annuity purchases require product terms, surrender
periods, fees, guarantees, insurer strength, tax treatment, and professional
review. The coach should not recommend a product or exchange.

**Employment, business, rental, and part-time income can bridge timing gaps.**
These sources need durability, tax, health insurance, and schedule assumptions.
Do not treat a hoped-for bridge income as guaranteed.

**Cash reserves and taxable savings manage transition risk.** Bank balances,
brokerage accounts, and expected large expenses determine whether the user can
absorb timing gaps, delayed benefits, healthcare costs, taxes, or market
volatility.

## Readiness Inventory

A useful income-source inventory captures:

- source name and owner;
- current status: unknown, missing statement, user-provided estimate, verified,
  or professional-review-needed;
- expected start window, if known;
- whether the amount is fixed, inflation-adjusted, market-dependent, or
  contract-dependent;
- tax treatment or tax-review need;
- survivor, beneficiary, or household dependency;
- source URL, statement date, plan document date, or professional source.

If a source cannot be verified, the plan should mark it as a data gap rather
than filling in an assumption.

## CashNerd Use

The retirement-income readiness artifact should combine this inventory with
current spending context and a handoff packet. It may use read-only household
data and deterministic education helpers for rough gap framing, but it should
label estimates as rough and block implementation decisions until facts are
verified.

Allowed output:

- "Your inventory is missing your SSA estimate and pension survivor options."
- "Ask the plan administrator whether this estimate includes a COLA."
- "Bring your IRA, 401(k), taxable brokerage, pension, annuity, and Social
  Security statements to the professional review."

Not allowed:

- "Claim Social Security at 67."
- "Take the pension lump sum."
- "Buy this annuity."
- "Withdraw from this account first."
- "Use this tax treatment on your return."

## Handoff Triggers

Route to a fiduciary planner, CPA, attorney, insurance professional, SHIP
counselor, or plan administrator when:

- the user wants an election, purchase, rollover, conversion, withdrawal, or
  claiming decision;
- tax treatment, RMDs, basis, withholding, or estimated taxes affect the plan;
- survivor benefits, divorce, trust ownership, beneficiary disputes, or estate
  terms matter;
- annuity, insurance, or pension contract language controls the answer;
- employer coverage or Medicare timing affects retirement timing.

## Common Pitfalls

- Treating a statement estimate as guaranteed income.
- Mixing nominal account balances with after-tax spendable income.
- Ignoring survivor, spouse, or household dependency.
- Treating Social Security, pensions, annuities, and portfolio withdrawals as
  interchangeable.
- Forgetting Medicare, health costs, taxes, and emergency reserves.
- Making an implementation recommendation before the income source is verified.
