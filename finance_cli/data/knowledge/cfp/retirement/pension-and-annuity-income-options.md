# Pension And Annuity Income Options

Pension and annuity readiness helps the user understand which contract or plan
facts they need before electing, buying, surrendering, exchanging, or relying on
income products. It is not a product recommendation, replacement analysis, or
pension-election answer.

Use this topic when a user mentions a pension estimate, lump-sum offer, annuity
contract, annuity quote, lifetime income illustration, surrender period, or
retirement income guarantee.

## Key Concepts

**Pensions and annuities are not generic balances.** A pension may promise a
formula-based benefit under plan terms. An annuity is an insurance contract that
may provide income, tax deferral, guarantees, market exposure, or riders under
contract terms.

**Lifetime income illustrations are estimates.** DOL EBSA describes retirement
plan lifetime income illustrations as showing how an account balance could
translate into estimated monthly payments. The illustration helps users think
about income, but it is not a guarantee of their final retirement income.

**Election choices can be irreversible or hard to reverse.** Single-life,
joint-and-survivor, period-certain, lump-sum, partial lump-sum, inflation
adjustment, survivor, and beneficiary choices can affect the household for life.
The coach should gather options and questions, not choose.

**Annuities have product-specific risks and costs.** Investor.gov distinguishes
fixed, indexed, registered index-linked, and variable annuities, with different
risk, fee, surrender, regulator, and market-exposure characteristics. Existing
contracts can include surrender charges, market value adjustments, tax effects,
and benefit reductions.

**Tax treatment needs source-specific review.** IRS Publication 575 covers
pension and annuity income for federal tax reporting. IRA annuity or IRA
distribution facts may point to Publication 590-B instead. Do not infer the
tax answer from the product label alone.

## Data To Gather

For pensions:

- plan name, administrator, and latest statement date;
- available election options;
- normal and early retirement dates;
- lump-sum amount, monthly benefit estimates, and survivor options;
- COLA or no-COLA status;
- spouse consent or beneficiary requirements;
- tax withholding and rollover options;
- deadline for election.

For annuities:

- insurer, contract type, issue date, and owner/annuitant;
- current value, surrender value, and surrender schedule;
- guarantees, riders, fees, caps, participation rates, or investment options;
- tax-qualified or nonqualified status;
- proposed exchange or purchase details;
- salesperson compensation or conflict questions;
- state insurance or SEC/FINRA complaint and registration context where
  relevant.

## Handoff Triggers

Route to:

- **fiduciary planner or RIA** for retirement-income fit, conflict review, and
  investment-adjacent product decisions;
- **insurance professional** for product mechanics, quotes, riders, state
  insurance questions, and insurer-specific terms;
- **CPA** for tax treatment, withholding, rollovers, basis, and reporting;
- **attorney** for spouse consent, divorce, beneficiary, trust, estate, or
  contract-dispute questions;
- **plan administrator** for pension option calculations and plan terms.

## Common Pitfalls

- Treating an illustration as guaranteed income.
- Comparing lump sums and monthly payments without survivor, tax, health, and
  longevity context.
- Ignoring surrender charges or new surrender periods.
- Confusing insurance guarantees with market returns.
- Missing salesperson compensation or replacement incentives.
- Treating state insurance and SEC-regulated features as one simple category.
