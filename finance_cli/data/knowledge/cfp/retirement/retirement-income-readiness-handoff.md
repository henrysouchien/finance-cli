# Retirement Income Readiness Handoff

A retirement income readiness handoff is the packet that separates useful
preparation from regulated implementation. It records the user's question,
known facts, missing facts, boundary response, professional type, and questions
to ask.

Use this topic inside `coach_retirement_income_readiness` whenever the user's
retirement-income question touches claiming, Medicare timing, RMDs,
withdrawals, pensions, annuities, tax, investments, legal documents, or
employer plan interpretation.

## Packet Sections

Include:

- **User question:** preserve the exact decision the user asked about.
- **Boundary response:** say what CashNerd can do now and what it is not
  answering.
- **Readiness status:** education-only, data-needed, inventory-ready,
  timing-review-needed, professional-review-needed, transition-ready, or refer.
- **Known facts:** age band, target retirement timing, employment coverage,
  household status, income sources, balances, spending, and documents.
- **Missing facts:** SSA statement, Medicare timing facts, plan documents,
  pension or annuity contract terms, prior-year balances, tax facts, or
  professional context.
- **Milestones:** Social Security claiming window, Medicare enrollment window,
  pension election deadline, RMD relevance, planned retirement date, and tax
  filing deadlines when source-backed.
- **Professional routing:** fiduciary, CPA, SHIP counselor, insurance
  professional, benefits team, plan administrator, attorney, SSA, Medicare.gov,
  or other official source.
- **Questions to ask:** concrete questions for each professional or agency.
- **Documents to gather:** statements, tax forms, plan documents, policy or
  annuity contracts, Medicare notices, employer coverage forms, and prior
  advice.
- **Next actions:** allowed preparation steps only.

## Boundary Rules

The packet must not contain:

- an exact Social Security claiming recommendation;
- a Medicare plan choice;
- an RMD calculation represented as final tax advice;
- a withdrawal-order implementation plan;
- a Roth conversion, QCD, rollover, or tax strategy recommendation;
- a pension election answer;
- an annuity purchase, surrender, exchange, or replacement recommendation;
- a portfolio, security, fund, or asset-allocation recommendation;
- legal document interpretation or beneficiary legal advice.

If the user asked for one of these answers, the packet should say the answer
requires professional or official-source review and then list the facts needed
for that review.

## Professional Routing

Use specific routing language:

- **fiduciary planner or RIA:** retirement-income fit, withdrawal strategy,
  investment-adjacent planning, annuity suitability, and overall coordination;
- **CPA or tax professional:** RMDs, withholding, estimated taxes, conversions,
  distribution tax treatment, basis, and filing positions;
- **SHIP counselor:** Medicare timing and unbiased Medicare counseling;
- **insurance professional:** annuity or insurance product mechanics, quotes,
  riders, insurer questions, and product-specific disclosures;
- **benefits team or plan administrator:** employer coverage, pension options,
  plan rules, and plan-document facts;
- **attorney:** ERISA disputes, divorce, trusts, beneficiary legal effect,
  incapacity, estate, and contract disputes;
- **SSA or Medicare.gov:** official enrollment, eligibility, and benefit timing
  facts.

The current KB specialist enum does not include every operational routing label;
artifact prose can still name agencies, benefits teams, and plan
administrators.

## CashNerd Use

The handoff packet is appropriate when the user's next useful step is not a
durable financial action but a prepared conversation. The skill may save the
packet after the user confirms the facts and boundary response. It should not
call retirement-target, transfer, reminder, investment, annuity, or sibling
artifact write tools in v0.1.

Allowed next actions:

- "Download your SSA estimate."
- "Ask your employer benefits team whether this coverage is active employer
  coverage or retiree coverage."
- "Bring this pension option sheet to a fiduciary and CPA."
- "Ask a SHIP counselor about your Medicare enrollment timing."

Not allowed next actions:

- "Claim now."
- "Enroll in this Medicare plan."
- "Take this RMD from this account."
- "Choose the lump sum."
- "Buy or surrender this annuity."

## Common Pitfalls

- Turning a handoff packet into a hidden recommendation.
- Omitting the original user question.
- Failing to distinguish verified facts from user-stated estimates.
- Listing a generic "advisor" when the issue belongs to a CPA, SHIP counselor,
  insurance professional, plan administrator, SSA, Medicare.gov, or attorney.
- Saving sensitive documents or legal communications instead of metadata.
- Treating the packet as implementation approval.
