# Social Security Claiming Readiness

Social Security claiming readiness means the user understands the variables
that affect a claiming decision and has gathered the facts needed for a
professional or official-source review. It does not mean CashNerd chooses the
claiming age.

Use this topic when the user asks when to claim, how claiming affects retirement
income, or what to gather before deciding.

## Key Concepts

**Eligibility and timing are separate.** SSA says retirement benefits can
typically start as early as age 62 for people who have worked and paid Social
Security taxes long enough, but starting before full retirement age generally
reduces the monthly benefit. Delaying after full retirement age can increase the
monthly benefit up to age 70.

**Full retirement age depends on birth year.** The coach should not calculate
full retirement age from memory. Use SSA source metadata or ask the user to
check SSA directly.

**The claiming decision is personal and household-specific.** SSA's publication
frames claiming around current cash needs, health, family longevity, work in
retirement, other income, future obligations, and survivor protection. The
coach should gather those facts rather than reduce the decision to a generic
break-even rule.

**Work and other benefits can matter.** Earnings before full retirement age,
spousal benefits, survivor benefits, dependent benefits, disability history,
government pensions, and tax treatment can change the analysis. These are
handoff signals when the user asks for an answer.

**Medicare timing is linked but distinct.** A user who delays Social Security
past 65 may still need to handle Medicare enrollment separately. Social Security
claiming readiness should flag Medicare timing instead of assuming one decision
automatically handles the other.

## Data To Gather

Ask for or route the user to gather:

- their current SSA estimate or statement;
- birth year and target retirement timing;
- whether they have already claimed any Social Security benefit;
- expected work income before and after claiming;
- spouse, former spouse, survivor, dependent, or disability benefit context;
- health, longevity, cash-flow, and survivor-protection concerns;
- other income sources and essential expenses;
- Medicare enrollment status or timing question.

If the user cannot provide an SSA estimate, mark the plan `data_needed`.

## CashNerd Use

Allowed output:

- explain early, full-retirement-age, and delayed claiming vocabulary;
- identify missing SSA facts;
- create questions for SSA, a fiduciary planner, or a benefits reviewer;
- compare the household facts that should be reviewed.

Not allowed:

- choose an exact claiming age;
- state a personalized optimal claiming strategy;
- recommend filing now or delaying;
- calculate a current benefit amount without source-backed SSA data;
- treat an illustrative example as the user's result.

## Handoff Triggers

Prepare a handoff when:

- the user asks "Should I claim now?" or asks CashNerd to choose an age;
- spousal, survivor, divorced-spouse, dependent, disability, or government
  pension facts are involved;
- the household has material longevity, health, debt, tax, or cash-flow
  pressure;
- Medicare enrollment timing is uncertain;
- the user's plan depends on exact monthly benefit amounts.

The handoff should preserve the user's question and list the missing facts. It
should not hide the claiming recommendation inside a question.

## Common Pitfalls

- Treating age 62, full retirement age, or age 70 as universally best.
- Ignoring survivor protection for couples.
- Forgetting work earnings before full retirement age.
- Assuming Social Security and Medicare start together.
- Using break-even math without household, health, tax, and survivor context.
- Quoting current benefit amounts from memory instead of SSA statements.
