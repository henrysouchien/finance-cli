# Medicare Enrollment Readiness

Medicare enrollment readiness helps the user know which timing facts and
coverage questions they need before retiring or delaying Social Security. It is
not Medicare plan selection, underwriting advice, or a coverage
recommendation.

Use this topic when retirement timing, employer coverage, COBRA, Marketplace
coverage, HSA eligibility, or Social Security delay could affect Medicare
enrollment.

## Key Concepts

**First eligibility is usually around age 65.** Medicare.gov states that most
people sign up for both Part A and Part B when first eligible, usually when
they turn 65. The initial sign-up window generally starts 3 months before the
month a person turns 65 and ends 3 months after that month.

**Late enrollment can create coverage gaps or penalties.** Medicare.gov warns
that missing an enrollment period can mean waiting, going without coverage, and
possibly paying a monthly penalty. The skill should flag this risk without
calculating a penalty from memory.

**Employer coverage changes the question.** For some people with current
job-based coverage from their own or a spouse's active employment, a Special
Enrollment Period may apply. Medicare.gov distinguishes active employer
coverage from COBRA, retiree coverage, Marketplace coverage, VA, TRICARE,
Medicaid, ESRD, disability, and other special cases.

**Part A and Part B can have different tradeoffs.** Many people do not pay a
premium for Part A, while Part B generally has a premium. HSA eligibility,
employer coverage, and retirement timing can affect the right questions to ask.

**Coverage start dates are operational.** The user may need to sign up before
job-based coverage ends if they want Medicare coverage to begin at a specific
time. Direct them to Medicare.gov, Social Security, SHIP, or the employer
benefits team for exact timing.

## Data To Gather

Capture:

- current age band and target retirement date;
- whether the user already has any Medicare parts;
- current health coverage type;
- whether the coverage is from active employment, retiree coverage, COBRA,
  Marketplace coverage, Medicaid, VA, CHAMPVA, TRICARE, or another source;
- whether the user or spouse is still working for the employer providing
  coverage;
- employer-size facts if Medicare.gov asks for them;
- HSA contribution status;
- desired coverage start month;
- whether the user wants plan choice help, which routes outside this skill.

## CashNerd Use

Allowed output:

- identify likely Medicare timing questions to check;
- flag that Social Security delay does not remove Medicare enrollment work;
- create a SHIP, Medicare.gov, SSA, employer-benefits, or insurance-agent
  question list;
- save timing status as unknown, review-needed, or handoff-needed.

Not allowed:

- choose Original Medicare, Medicare Advantage, Medigap, Part D, or a carrier;
- compare specific plans or formularies;
- calculate penalties without current official-source inputs;
- interpret employer plan documents;
- tell the user to keep or stop HSA contributions as a final instruction.

## Handoff Triggers

Prepare a handoff when:

- the user is near age 65 and has employer, COBRA, Marketplace, VA, TRICARE,
  Medicaid, or retiree coverage;
- HSA contributions are active or planned near Medicare enrollment;
- the user asks which Medicare plan to choose;
- coverage start date, penalty exposure, or coordination with employer coverage
  is unclear;
- a spouse or disabled family-member coverage rule is involved.

Use SHIP counselor for impartial Medicare counseling, insurance agent for plan
product questions, employer benefits team for employer coverage facts, and SSA
or Medicare.gov for enrollment timing.

## Common Pitfalls

- Assuming Social Security and Medicare decisions happen together.
- Treating COBRA or retiree coverage as the same as active employer coverage.
- Ignoring HSA timing.
- Waiting until after employer coverage ends to ask about Part B timing.
- Choosing a Medicare plan inside a general retirement readiness workflow.
- Quoting premium, penalty, or IRMAA amounts from memory.

## Effective-Date Notice

This topic is enrollment-readiness framing. Enrollment windows, coverage start
dates, penalties, premiums, special enrollment periods, HSA interactions, and
plan options can change or depend on facts. Use current Medicare.gov, SSA,
SHIP, employer, or plan-source information before giving operational timing.
