# End-of-Life Planning

End-of-life planning covers documents and conversations for incapacity. It answers who can make medical or financial decisions if the user cannot communicate, what preferences the user wants known, and where documents can be found in an emergency.

For CashNerd, this topic is a readiness and referral frame. The coach may help a user understand document categories, identify whether documents exist, prepare questions and conversations, and track a review checklist. The coach does not draft, interpret, validate, summarize, or store legal-document contents.

## Key Concepts

**Advance directive or living will.** An advance directive records medical-treatment preferences for situations where the user cannot speak for themselves. Terminology, form requirements, witness/notary rules, and triggering conditions vary by state.

**Healthcare proxy or medical power of attorney.** A healthcare proxy names someone to make medical decisions when the user lacks capacity. The core coaching task is preparation: who the user trusts, whether the person is willing, whether alternates exist, and what conversations the user should have.

**Financial power of attorney.** A power of attorney authorizes an agent to act on financial or legal matters. A durable power of attorney is designed to continue through incapacity. Whether a document is durable, springing, broad, narrow, accepted by institutions, or appropriate for the user's facts is attorney work.

**DNR, DNI, POLST, MOLST, or similar medical orders.** These are medical-order or medical-instruction surfaces that vary by state and clinical context. They should be discussed with medical professionals and, when part of estate planning, coordinated with legal documents.

**Document access matters.** A document that exists but cannot be found or used in an emergency may fail the practical readiness test. The coach can prompt storage and access questions without storing document bodies, credentials, or private medical details.

## Document Readiness Use

Use this topic in `coach_estate_document_readiness` to structure metadata-only status checks for:

- healthcare proxy or medical power of attorney;
- advance directive or living will;
- financial power of attorney or durable power of attorney;
- HIPAA release or other medical-information access document when relevant;
- emergency contacts and alternates;
- document storage and who knows where documents are;
- recent life events that may make named people or preferences stale.

Allowed status values should stay administrative: `present`, `missing`, `unknown`, `stale`, `needs_attorney_review`, or `not_applicable`. Do not ask the user to paste medical directives, powers of attorney, signatures, passwords, medical records, or attorney communications.

## Proxy Conversation Preparation

The coach can help the user prepare conversations with named people:

- confirm the person is willing to serve;
- identify alternates if the first person cannot serve;
- discuss values and preferences at a high level;
- make sure the person knows documents exist and where to find them;
- identify whether family conflict, distance, age, or practical availability creates a review need;
- prepare questions for an estate attorney or medical professional.

The coach should not decide who the user should name or what medical treatment instructions should say.

## Life-Event Review Triggers

Prompt for review when the user reports:

- marriage, divorce, separation, or remarriage;
- death, incapacity, estrangement, relocation, or unavailability of a named agent or proxy;
- move to a different state;
- new diagnosis, major health change, pregnancy, or caregiving role;
- major change in religious, cultural, or personal preferences;
- long-term-care planning;
- a long time since last review, especially when the user cannot remember the date.

## Hand-Off Discipline

The coach may:

- explain document categories and role vocabulary;
- help the user inventory what exists;
- help the user prepare questions for an attorney or medical professional;
- help the user list people who may need to know where documents are;
- track checklist metadata and review dates.

The coach must route out for:

- drafting, editing, reviewing, or interpreting advance directives, living wills, healthcare proxies, powers of attorney, DNR/DNI/POLST/MOLST forms, or HIPAA releases;
- deciding who should be named as healthcare proxy, financial agent, or alternate;
- determining whether an existing document is valid, durable, accepted by an institution, or effective in a state;
- advising what medical treatments the user should accept or refuse;
- resolving family disputes, capacity disputes, guardianship/conservatorship issues, or medical-provider conflicts.

## Common Pitfalls

- Assuming incapacity documents are only for older adults.
- Naming a proxy or agent without telling them.
- Naming someone who is unavailable, unwilling, conflicted, or no longer in the user's life.
- Signing a non-durable power of attorney when the user expected incapacity coverage.
- Moving states and never reviewing documents.
- Storing documents somewhere agents or family cannot access.
- Treating DNR/DNI/POLST-style instructions as substitutes for a broader planning package.
- Asking an AI coach to review legal document language instead of taking it to an attorney.

This topic is evergreen readiness framing with state-law and clinical-context sensitivity.
