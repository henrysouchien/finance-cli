# Form ADV and Advisor Due Diligence

Form ADV is a core document set for reviewing an investment adviser. CashNerd
can explain what the form is, point users to the kinds of information it
contains, and prepare a due diligence checklist. CashNerd should not rate,
approve, or recommend a specific adviser as the user's answer.

## Form ADV Basics

Investor.gov describes Form ADV as the uniform form investment advisers use to
register with the SEC and state securities authorities. It includes information
about business practices, fees, conflicts of interest, disciplinary information,
and client relationship summaries.

Common parts:

- **Part 1:** registration and business information used by regulators.
- **Part 2:** brochure information about services, fees, conflicts, business
  practices, and disciplinary disclosures.
- **Part 3:** relationship summary designed to help retail investors compare
  services, fees, conflicts, and conduct questions.

Users can use regulator lookup tools to review an adviser's registration and
disclosures. CashNerd can prepare the checklist, but the user or a professional
must evaluate whether the adviser is appropriate.

## Due Diligence Questions

Prepare questions such as:

- Are you acting as a fiduciary for this engagement?
- What services are included, and what is outside scope?
- How are you compensated: asset-based fee, hourly, flat fee, commission,
  referral fee, product compensation, or another model?
- What conflicts of interest could affect this recommendation?
- Do you have disciplinary disclosures, complaints, or legal history I should
  review?
- Will you have discretion over accounts or require custody of assets?
- Which documents should I read before signing?
- How often will we monitor or update the plan?

These questions are educational and due-diligence oriented. They are not a
recommendation to hire or avoid a named adviser.

## CashNerd Use

For `coach_advisor_handoff_readiness`, include a Form ADV checklist when the
professional type is an RIA, CFP professional offering investment advice, or
unknown investment professional. The packet can record:

- the adviser's name only if supplied by the user;
- what lookup or disclosure document the user should review;
- questions about fees, conflicts, scope, services, and disciplinary history;
- open concerns the user wants to discuss.

Do not scrape, summarize, or rank a specific Form ADV filing unless a future
release explicitly approves that workflow and its legal review.

## Common Pitfalls

- Treating a clean checklist as a hiring recommendation.
- Assuming CFP, RIA, broker, CPA, and insurance roles share the same disclosure
  form.
- Omitting compensation and conflict questions.
- Ignoring disciplinary disclosures because the user likes the professional's
  marketing copy.
- Letting an affiliate or paid-referral relationship appear neutral.
