# Conflicts, Compensation, and Referrals

Advice boundaries are not only about the content of the recommendation. They
also depend on who gets paid, how they get paid, what conflicts exist, and
whether a referral or endorsement is compensated. CashNerd should make those
questions visible before sending a user to a professional or partner.

This topic is process guidance. It does not approve solicitor, promoter,
testimonial, endorsement, affiliate, or referral-compensation programs.

## Conflict and Compensation Vocabulary

**Material conflict.** A conflict is material when it could reasonably affect
the professional's advice, recommendations, or objectivity. The user should know
what the conflict is and how it is managed.

**Sales-related compensation.** Compensation can include commissions, product
revenue, referral or solicitor fees, bonuses, sales awards, or other economic
benefits connected to a user's decision.

**Referral compensation.** A person or company may receive an economic benefit
for referring a user to another provider. That can create a conflict even when
the referred provider is qualified.

**Promoter, testimonial, or endorsement activity.** SEC marketing-rule materials
address compensated testimonials, endorsements, disclosure, oversight, written
agreement, and disqualification conditions for investment adviser advertising
contexts. CashNerd should treat paid referral and endorsement designs as legal
review territory.

## CashNerd Use

Handoff packets should surface questions such as:

- How are you compensated for this engagement?
- Do you receive commissions, product compensation, referral fees, or other
  economic benefits?
- Are you affiliated with the product, custodian, platform, insurer, lender, or
  professional being recommended?
- What conflicts of interest apply, and how are they managed?
- Are you acting as a fiduciary for this engagement?
- Will CashNerd, an affiliate, or any partner receive compensation if I proceed?

If CashNerd has referral or partner metadata for the flow, the artifact should
include a disclosure flag. Do not bury paid-referral context in logs or internal
metadata.

## Product Boundary

For v0.1 advisor handoff readiness:

- prepare facts, questions, and disclosure prompts;
- do not implement a referral marketplace;
- do not recommend a named professional because of compensation;
- do not present paid placements as neutral results;
- route compensation-program design to legal and compliance review.

## Common Pitfalls

- Treating compensation disclosure as optional because the user asked for a
  quick recommendation.
- Saying "no conflicts" without asking how the professional or platform is paid.
- Recommending a partner first and explaining the compensation later.
- Using affiliate links or referral codes inside an advice-shaped handoff packet
  without a user-visible disclosure.
- Letting marketing copy substitute for professional due diligence.
