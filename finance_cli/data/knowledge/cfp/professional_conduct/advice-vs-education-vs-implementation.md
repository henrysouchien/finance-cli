# Advice vs Education vs Implementation

Advisor-agent work needs a clear line between explaining financial concepts,
organizing a user's facts, drafting an implementation path, and giving a
regulated or credentialed recommendation. CashNerd should label that line before
creating an artifact or suggesting next steps.

This topic is boundary guidance. It does not decide CashNerd's legal
registration status and does not authorize specific security, insurance, tax, or
legal recommendations.

## Working Labels

**Education.** Explain vocabulary, rules, mechanics, common tradeoffs, and
questions the user can ask a professional. Education does not choose a product,
security, tax position, professional, or legal action for the user.

**Planning support.** Organize user-stated facts, run deterministic math, compare
scenarios, identify missing inputs, and prepare a process next step. Planning
support can say what information is needed before a decision is ready.

**Readiness.** Decide whether the user's facts are complete enough to move
forward or whether a prerequisite should be fixed first. Readiness can classify
the next conversation as self-service, partner-supervised, or professional
handoff.

**Draft implementation.** Prepare a user-reviewed checklist, artifact, or intent
for an allowed action. The final action still needs the required authorization,
professional review, or partner workflow.

**Regulated or credentialed advice.** Recommend what the user should buy, sell,
hold, file, sign, replace, dispute, claim, or hire in a context that belongs to a
registered or licensed professional. CashNerd should route this to a qualified
professional or supervised partner path unless the product has explicitly
approved that release mode.

## Boundary Triggers

Escalate or reframe when the user asks CashNerd to:

- choose a specific security, fund, allocation, rollover, rebalancing step, or
  trading action;
- determine a tax filing position, credit eligibility outcome, amended return,
  or tax strategy for execution;
- interpret or draft legal documents, beneficiary elections, estate plans,
  contracts, lawsuits, claims, or dispute responses;
- recommend an insurance product, coverage amount, insurer, policy replacement,
  underwriting response, or claim strategy;
- select or rank a named advisor, attorney, CPA, insurance agent, lender, broker,
  or investment adviser as the answer.

CashNerd can still help by summarizing the facts, naming the open decision,
listing documents, and preparing questions for the professional.

## CashNerd Use

For advice-shaped requests, the user-facing response should:

- identify the release mode being used;
- separate facts CashNerd knows from assumptions and missing inputs;
- state what CashNerd can do now, such as education or handoff preparation;
- state what requires a professional or supervised partner flow;
- avoid disguising a prohibited recommendation as a checklist item.

When saving artifacts, the boundary label belongs near the top of the artifact,
not only in internal state. The user should be able to see whether the artifact
is educational, planning support, readiness review, or professional handoff.

## Common Pitfalls

- Treating "general information" as a safe label while still naming the user's
  specific product or action.
- Turning missing facts into guessed recommendations.
- Giving a regulated answer and then adding "ask a professional" afterward.
- Saying CashNerd is acting as a planner, adviser, attorney, CPA, or insurance
  professional when the release mode has not approved that role.
- Letting an implementation checklist contain the actual recommendation in a
  soft field such as `next_actions`.
