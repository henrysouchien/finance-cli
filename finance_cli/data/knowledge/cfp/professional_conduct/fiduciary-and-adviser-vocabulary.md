# Fiduciary and Adviser Vocabulary

Users often ask for "an advisor" without knowing whether they need a CFP
professional, investment adviser, broker, CPA, attorney, insurance professional,
or another specialist. CashNerd can explain the vocabulary and prepare due
diligence questions, but it should not convert vocabulary into a specific
professional-selection recommendation.

## Core Terms

**Fiduciary.** A fiduciary duty generally means acting in the client's best
interest within the applicable engagement and legal standard. CFP professionals
owe fiduciary duties when providing financial advice under CFP Board standards.
Other professionals may have different duties based on their role, law,
registration, contract, and scope.

**Investment adviser.** Investor.gov describes investment advisers as people or
firms paid for advice about securities. They may provide ongoing advice about
buying, selling, or holding investments, and users should review services, fees,
conflicts, and legal or disciplinary history.

**Broker or registered representative.** A broker-dealer professional may make
securities recommendations under broker-dealer rules and other applicable
standards. FINRA Rule 2111 describes a suitability obligation for covered
recommendations and requires reasonable diligence about the customer and the
recommended transaction or strategy. FINRA also notes that Rule 2111 is not the
same as Regulation Best Interest recommendations.

**CFP professional.** A CFP professional is subject to CFP Board standards when
providing financial advice and may also be registered or licensed in other ways.
The credential does not erase the need to understand scope, compensation, and
conflicts.

**CPA, attorney, and insurance professional.** These roles cover tax,
accounting, legal, and insurance implementation questions that often appear
inside financial planning. Their authority and obligations depend on licensing,
jurisdiction, engagement scope, and product context.

## CashNerd Use

CashNerd can help the user:

- identify which professional type is relevant to the question;
- understand questions to ask about fiduciary status, registration, fees,
  conflicts, custody, discretion, and scope;
- separate investment advice from tax, legal, insurance, and planning process
  questions;
- prepare facts and documents before meeting a professional.

CashNerd should not:

- state that a named person is the right professional for the user;
- imply every professional using "advisor" is held to the same standard;
- label CashNerd itself as a fiduciary without product-approved release mode and
  legal review;
- use fiduciary vocabulary to give the underlying regulated recommendation.

## Handoff Discipline

When the user needs a human professional, the handoff packet should say what
type of review is needed and why. Example: "This is a securities allocation
question, so the packet is prepared for a fiduciary investment adviser or CFP
professional to review." It should not say: "Hire this adviser" or "This adviser
is best for you."
