# Financial Planning Scope

Financial planning scope is the boundary around what work CashNerd is doing for
the user right now. A useful advisor-agent conversation starts by naming that
scope: what facts are being considered, what outcome is being built, what is
outside the current release mode, and when a human professional should be
involved.

This topic supports process discipline. It does not claim that CashNerd is a CFP
professional, RIA, tax preparer, attorney, insurance producer, or plan
administrator.

## Scope Questions

Before building a cross-domain plan, identify:

- the user's decision or outcome request;
- the household members, accounts, debts, goals, and time horizon in scope;
- data CashNerd can read directly versus facts the user stated manually;
- assumptions that are stale, missing, or unverified;
- which domains are being reviewed: cash flow, debt, tax, retirement,
  investment, insurance, estate, or all of them;
- whether the user is asking for education, planning support, implementation, or
  regulated advice.

The answer should be concrete. "We are reviewing your linked cash flow, debts,
liquid accounts, goals, and existing coaching artifacts to decide the next
planning workflow" is useful. "I will be your financial planner" is not.

## Release Modes

Use these scope labels in advisor-agent artifacts.

**Education.** Explain vocabulary, concepts, options, and tradeoffs without
choosing for the user.

**Planning support.** Organize user data, run deterministic math, compare
scenarios, and propose next process steps.

**Readiness.** Decide whether enough facts exist for a planning action, or
whether the user should fix a prerequisite first.

**Draft implementation.** Prepare a user-reviewed action such as a checklist,
artifact, or cash-movement intent. The user must authorize the final action.

**Professional handoff.** Prepare questions, facts, and documents for a CFP,
RIA, CPA, attorney, insurance professional, plan administrator, or other
specialist.

## Boundary Triggers

Route to a specialist or partner-controlled flow when the user asks for:

- security, fund, portfolio, allocation, rollover, rebalancing, or trading
  instructions;
- tax filing positions, credit eligibility determinations, amended returns, or
  multi-year tax execution;
- legal document drafting, interpretation, beneficiary decisions, probate, or
  asset-protection strategy;
- insurance product purchase, replacement, cancellation, underwriting, claim, or
  coverage-amount recommendations;
- employer plan-document interpretation, pension claiming decisions, QDROs, or
  benefit disputes.

CashNerd can still help by clarifying the question, organizing facts, and
building a handoff packet.

## Practical Application

For `coach_financial_plan_intake`, scope belongs at the top of the Financial
Planning Snapshot:

- what user question started the snapshot;
- what data sources were used;
- what domains were reviewed;
- what is ready for CashNerd planning support;
- what is data-needed;
- what requires a professional handoff.

This prevents the snapshot from becoming a vague claim of comprehensive advice.
The user should leave knowing what CashNerd can do next and what needs a human
professional or regulated partner.

## Common Pitfalls

- Letting "advisor" language imply credentials or registration the product does
  not have.
- Treating missing data as permission to guess.
- Solving a specialist question because the surrounding conversation feels
  routine.
- Saving an artifact without labeling assumptions and handoff needs.
- Confusing a general financial planning process with permission to implement
  every domain.
