# Advisor Handoff Packet

An advisor handoff packet is a fact and question container for a human
professional. It helps the user arrive prepared without pretending CashNerd has
already made the professional's recommendation.

The packet can be complete within CashNerd's release mode even when the final
answer belongs to a CFP professional, investment adviser, CPA, attorney,
insurance professional, or other specialist.

## Packet Sections

Include:

- **User request:** the exact decision or question the user asked.
- **Release mode:** education, planning support, referral/handoff,
  partner-supervised, or registered/in-house required.
- **Boundary response:** what CashNerd can do now and what it is not answering.
- **Professional type:** likely reviewer type and the reason for that routing.
- **Known facts:** relevant balances, goals, debts, cash flow, policies,
  documents, deadlines, and user-stated constraints.
- **Relevant artifacts:** links or names of CashNerd artifacts the professional
  should review.
- **Missing inputs:** facts the user should gather before the meeting.
- **Questions to ask:** professional, compensation, conflict, fiduciary, Form
  ADV, tax, legal, insurance, or plan-administrator questions.
- **Documents to bring:** statements, policies, tax forms, plan documents,
  notices, contracts, or prior advice.
- **Disclosures to surface:** scope boundary, referral compensation, conflict of
  interest, partner relationship, or no known referral compensation.
- **Next actions:** allowed preparation steps only.

## Boundary Rules

The packet should not contain:

- a specific security, fund, portfolio, rollover, or trading recommendation;
- a tax filing position or legal strategy decision;
- an insurance product, coverage amount, insurer, policy replacement, or claim
  strategy recommendation;
- a named professional ranking or hiring recommendation;
- a paid-referral call to action that lacks disclosure;
- unsupported claims that CashNerd is acting as a credentialed or registered
  professional.

If the user needs the professional answer, the packet should say so directly and
preserve the question for the human reviewer.

## CashNerd Use

For `coach_advisor_handoff_readiness`, save the packet only after the release
mode, professional type, known facts, missing inputs, and user-facing boundary
response are explicit. If the user asked for a prohibited answer, record the
refused topic and the allowed help CashNerd provided.

The packet can still be useful when data is incomplete. In that case, mark the
handoff as data-needed and list the missing inputs instead of manufacturing an
answer.

## Common Pitfalls

- Saving a packet that contains the prohibited recommendation in a summary field.
- Omitting conflict and compensation questions for investment or referral flows.
- Turning a professional type into a named-provider recommendation.
- Failing to show which facts are user-stated and unverified.
- Treating the handoff packet as proof that implementation is already approved.
